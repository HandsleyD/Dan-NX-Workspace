History of Pensions From Railway Workers to Modern Times

Discover how DB pensions in the UK evolved from early 20th-century railway schemes to become a cornerstone of British employment, and why they've become increasingly rare today.

The Origins Story

How DB pensions began in Britain

1900s

Railway Pioneers

DB pensions in the UK first started in the early twentieth century. Railway companies were among the first to set up formal pension schemes for their employees. These early schemes recognized the physical demands of railway work and the need for dignified retirement.

1936

Early Foundations

By 1936, approximately 2.6 million workers were covered by DB schemes, primarily in the public sector and among large employers who could afford the administrative complexity.
